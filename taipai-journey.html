Day 01

@Taipei

A few days ago I was sat on the beach in Bali with Lauren, Jacob, and Hayley when we all decided to spontaneously book a trip to a new destination; Taipei! None of us knew anything about this place so we were pretty excited to explore! We arrived late at night & checked into our hotel (Palais de Chine) a really cool hotel with an almost gothic / quirky vibe. We spent our first day walking around the city indulging in good food, shopping, art exhibitions, & ended with the famous Shilim markets!

Day 01

@Shilin Night Market

The Shilin night market was so much fun! We spent hours playing random games (most of the time loosing) but between us all we won 4 prizes which we were quite proud of haha. We ended the night at around 10pm and immediately crashed after a long but awesome first day! I’m excited to see what tomorrow brings!

Day 02

@Taipei

After a bit of research we decided to start the dayoff with breakfast at a healthy cafe called ‘untitled’ known for delicious avo toast! The food was amazing and the black tea art was really cool. However if you’re in a hurry I wouldn’t recommend as they take a longgg time to bring out the food! Worth it though if you do have time!

Day 02

The weather was pretty terrible so we decided to take shelter at the 101 shopping mall for the rest of the day. Since we didn’t have much of a cultural breakfast we decided to hit up a dumpling restaurant for lunch. This place was super busy and had a continuous waiting list, but for a good reason - the food was incredible!

Day 02

We ended the night with some cocktails at a members only bar called staff only. It’s a really cool bar located in a back street. There’s no signs or anything so unless you’re looking for it you wouldn’t stumble upon the place. After entering we were taken upstairs to the quirky seating area where we ordered our drinks and a few snacks. The drinks were tasty but very strong, which is always a good thing!

Day 03

@Taipei City

Today was a bit of a write off - after our crazy night out we spent the whole day in bed! We forced ourselves out of the hotel room by around 6pm (by then the sun had already set.) We met up with a couple of local photographers and went to a back street with a perfect view of the 101 tower. One of the locals had some light up masks which were cool as a photo prop! After that we all went back to bed haha, it was a bit of a wasted day but ah well it’s sometimes needed!

Day 04

@Taipei

Today was spent shopping and trying out more food (we couldn’t resist stopping by our favourite avo toast cafe again haha)

Day 04

@Taipei

At around 5pm we decided to take a hike up the famous elephant mountain for sunset! The walk wasn’t too crazy (only around 400 steps) but it was super humid which made it almost unbearable haha. By the time we reached the top we were dripping in sweat, worth it for the sunset view though!

Day 05

@Jioufen

Today we drove out of the city for 45 minutes to head to a place called Jioufen. This place was really cool, full of temples, views over the ocean, cultural streets and interesting food!
